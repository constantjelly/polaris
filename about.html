SPACE FACTS

From the scorching surface of the Sun to the icy depths of the Oort Cloud, the universe is teeming with phenomena that defy imagination. Every star you see is a sun. Every galaxy holds hundreds of billions of them. Our mission is to distil this infinite complexity into facts you can carry with you.

STARRY NIGHTS

Night skies hold a beauty that has moved poets, navigators, and dreamers for millennia. Our gallery collects the finest astrophotography from around the world — the Milky Way rising over desert dunes, aurora borealis shimmering over Arctic fjords, and meteor showers painting light across high-altitude plateaux.

EXAMS & SCHOLARSHIPS

Space science is one of humanity's greatest pursuits — and education is the launchpad. We curate a comprehensive database of astronomy-related examinations, research fellowships, and scholarships open to students at every level. Your journey to the stars can begin today.

FASCINATING FACTS

A few of our favourite wonders from across the cosmos.

THE SUN'S CORE

At 15 million degrees Celsius, the Sun's core fuses 620 million tonnes of hydrogen every single second, generating the energy that sustains all life on Earth.

THE MOON'S DISTANCE

The Moon is gradually drifting away from Earth at about 3.8 cm per year — roughly the same rate your fingernails grow.

EARTH'S BLUE HUE

Seen from space, Earth appears as a luminous blue marble — a result of oceans covering 71% of its surface, reflecting sunlight back into the cosmos.

LIGHT TRAVEL TIME

Light from the Sun takes 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth. From Proxima Centauri, the nearest star, it takes over 4 years.
